Customer Pain & Resolution

Map the pain directly to IGEL outcomes

Use these bullets in discovery & your pitch
Pain 1 · SaaS risk explosion

Growing SaaS usage is increasing security risk

Competitive Positioning

How to compare IGEL against alternatives

Contrast quickly

IGEL vs. Windows

  • • Windows is powerful but heavy and high-maintenance for simple SaaS workflows.
  • • IGEL is lightweight, secure, and purpose-built for SaaS access.
  • • Eliminates patching complexity and reduces malware exposure at the endpoint.

IGEL vs. ChromeOS

  • • IGEL runs on existing hardware — no full device refresh required.
  • • Offers more flexible enterprise management and integration.
  • • Supports richer authentication and control scenarios than a pure browser OS.

IGEL vs. VDI / DaaS

  • • For SaaS-first roles, full virtual desktops add cost and complexity.
  • • IGEL + SaaS delivers needed apps without VDI licensing overhead.
  • • Great fit where only occasional legacy Windows apps are required.

Common Objections

Objection handling crib sheet

Answer crisply

Objection

“We manage everything through Windows today.”

Rebuttal

Windows is powerful — but often overkill for simple SaaS workflows. IGEL reduces the attack surface, simplifies management, and cuts cost while keeping the user’s day-to-day SaaS workflow unchanged.

Objection

“Our hardware is too old.”

Rebuttal

IGEL is designed to repurpose older hardware and still deliver high performance for browser-based work. Many customers extend device life 3–5 years using IGEL instead of refreshing to run a heavy desktop OS.

Objection

“We already use MDM.”

Rebuttal

MDM manages devices; IGEL manages the OS itself. That means deeper control, a standardized user experience, and an endpoint with far fewer local OS vulnerabilities. MDM and IGEL are complementary, not redundant.

Objection

“We need logging and audit trails.”

Rebuttal

IGEL integrates with enterprise logging, monitoring, and SIEM tools so security teams get full auditability across IGEL-managed devices — aligned with their existing stacks.

Sales Motion

Repeatable SaaS / web app sales motion

Flow to follow

  1. 1 · Discovery & qualification

    Understand SaaS workflows, endpoint sprawl, and security concerns. Confirm they are SaaS-first with real challenges managing browsers and endpoints.

  2. 2 · IGEL value overview

    Deliver a tight pitch around secure SaaS access, simplified endpoint management, and cost reduction via hardware reuse.

  3. 3 · Tailored demo

    Show secure browser sessions, policy enforcement, and centralized control via UMS or IGEL cloud-based management.

  4. 4 · Pilot / PoC

    Convert 5–10 devices, run key SaaS apps (Salesforce, M365, etc.), and validate performance, UX, and management simplicity.

  5. 5 · ROI & business case

    Quantify cost avoidance from Windows licensing and hardware refresh, ticket reduction, and improved security posture.

  6. 6 · Contract & scale-out

    Propose per-device subscription, rollout plan, and training for IT and support teams to operationalize IGEL.
